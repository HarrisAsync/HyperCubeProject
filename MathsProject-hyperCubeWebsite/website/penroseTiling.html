Penrose Tiling


Named after the mathematician and physicist Roger Penrose, the Penrose Tiling is an example of an aperiodic tiling. Aperiodic tiling are a style of non-periodic tiling (tiles the plane in a non-repeating manner) which also possess the property of having no arbitrarily large periodic sections contained within it. Simply put, if tracing paper was used to copy the tiling pattern, if you were to move the paper, you would never be able to match the patterns (of the orignal tiling and tracing paper) despite direction or orientation.
Penrose tilings are formed with only 2 shapes, typically referred to a kite and a dart.
