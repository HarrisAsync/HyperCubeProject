Fibonacci Flower

The Fibonacci sequence is a pattern formed by the addition of the previous two numbers in the sucession (beginning with 0 and 1). It is found throughout the natural world and the spiral pattern on sunflower seeds is no exception. If the spirals are counted in a consistant manner from the centre outwards a fibonacci sequence will always emerge. 137.5° is approximately the golden angle of the spiral in which the Fibonacci flower is produced. For more information check out this Mathologer video.
The buds within the flower are generated by the following equations, where n is an increasing natural number and angle is the input angle:
a = n × angle , r = √n
x = r × cos(a) , y = r × sin(a)
The spiral line paths are determined by a simple shortest distance algorithm, and thus it fails to show all possible sprials. Toggle off Show spirals to find your own sprials by clicking on the buds.
