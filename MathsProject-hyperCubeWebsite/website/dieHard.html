The Die Hard problem

This particular problem was featured in the 1995 film Die Hard With a Vengeance , where the antagonist Simon Gruber rigs an explosive underneath a fountain and it is up to the protagonists John McClane and Zeus Carver to solve it. The problem involves two water jugs, one that can contain 5 gallons of water and the other having a capacity of 3 gallons. The bomb will deactivate if John and Zeus can fill one of the jugs with exactly 4 gallons of water.
One way of visualising this problem is by using a parallelogram-shaped billiards table. By using the bottom right corner of the table as the origin and the top left corner as the exit. We now shoot a ball located at the lower right corner as indicated. As the ball travels across the table, we note down the coordinates of the points on the upper and lower rail in the order that the ball bounces off them.
When referring to the exact problem in the Die Hard film, the dimensions of the table are 5 by 3 units, this sequence of coordinates corresponds to a solution of our problem as follows:
(5,0) = fill the 5-gallon jug
(2,3) = fill the 3-gallon jug from the 5-gallon jug, leaving 2 gallons in the 5-gallon jug.
(2,0) = empty the 3-gallon jug
(0,2) = empty the two gallons in the 5-gallon jug into the 3-gallon jug.
(5,2) = fill the 5-gallon jug, etc.
(4,3) = fill the 3-gallon jug from the 5-gallon jug, leaving 4 gallons in the 5-gallon jug.
